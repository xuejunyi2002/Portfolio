Key Challenges

Problem → Response → Outcome

Challenge
My Response
Outcome

01 — Networking

Client framework introduced unexpected dependencies across gameplay systems

Shifted to integration-first workflow. Increased client comms frequency and restructured sprints around ownership-based tracking.

Multiplayer loop stabilized while maintaining delivery milestones.

02 — Scope

Original ambitions exceeded what 12 weeks of multiplayer VR could deliver

Protected the core cooperative loop. Cut secondary features. Prioritized onboarding quality and multiplayer stability over quantity.

Cohesive vertical slice — not an overextended feature set.

03 — Coordination

VR interaction, UI, gameplay, and art pipelines were tightly interdependent

Ran continuous cross-functional check-ins. Improved transparency through ownership tracking and milestone dashboards.

Team maintained alignment and progression toward delivery targets.

Onboarding

Redesigned

First-time users needed stronger cross-role cues — full onboarding redesign shipped after Round 2.

Round 1 (Internal): Focused on onboarding clarity, VR comfort, combat usability, and UI readability. 5 design changes shipped before external testing.

Round 2 (External): Validated role interdependence and multiplayer comms flow. 6 design changes including warm rendering, organ toggle, and dome repositioning.

Production impact: Every testing round fed directly into sprint priorities — findings became tasks within 48 hours.

Leadership Reflection

What I'd Take Forward

01

Integration Risk Forecasting

Multiplayer complexity scaled faster than anticipated. Earlier dependency mapping would have allowed better buffer planning from Sprint 1.

02

Ownership-Based Structure from Day One

Shifting to explicit ownership tracking mid-project reduced blockers significantly — I'd build this in from the start on future projects.

03

Scalable Documentation

A structured documentation system from the beginning would reduce last-sprint overhead and improve handoff quality for the client.
