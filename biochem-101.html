Key Challenges

Problem → Response → Outcome

Challenge
My Response
Outcome
01 — Scope

New ideas kept expanding the workload beyond what 4 months of academic development could support

Ran frequent scope reassessment sessions focused on core pillars. Broke large tasks into sprint-sized goals and re-estimated timelines regularly throughout production.

Team maintained momentum and delivered a polished cooperative gameplay loop rather than an unfinished feature set.

02 — Disciplines

Designers, artists, and developers approached features from conflicting perspectives — stalling decisions and creating unclear implementation expectations

Acted as mediator between disciplines. Translated technical constraints into design considerations and ensured all perspectives were heard before decisions were finalized.

Reduced bottlenecks and improved collaboration throughout development — decisions moved faster with shared context.

03 — Co-op Readability

Early playtesters treated combat and chemistry systems as separate mechanics rather than cooperative interdependent tools

Iterated on gameplay structure and UI feedback based on playtest findings. Redesigned co-op interactions to encourage intentional coordination rather than parallel solo play.

Later playtests showed stronger player collaboration and improved understanding of the cooperative identity.

Leadership Reflection

What I'd Take Forward

01

Earlier Scope Guardrails

Scope kept creeping in early sprints. Setting explicit feature freeze checkpoints at the start would reduce the need for mid-project cuts under pressure.

02

Discipline Alignment Sessions

Cross-discipline friction was recurring. A structured kickoff alignment session — shared vocabulary, shared constraints — would reduce mediation overhead throughout the project.

03

Testing Earlier in the Cycle

Round 1 revealed issues that could have been caught sooner with lightweight paper prototyping. Earlier low-fidelity testing would accelerate the iteration loop.

Final Outcome

A playable co-op prototype built under real academic constraints — on time, with a team.
