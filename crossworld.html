Key Challenges

Problem → Response → Outcome

An ambitious VR multiplayer concept, a 13-week timeline, and a lot of ideas. My focus was to define scope, align disciplines, and protect the core experience.

01

Scope Control

Challenge

The original concept had more features than a 13-week multiplayer VR production could realistically deliver.

My Response

Defined the MVP around the core loop: VR combat, Strategist turrets, resource management, and win/loss conditions. Cut secondary features to keep production focused.

Outcome

Delivered a complete multiplayer loop as a cohesive vertical slice, not an overextended feature set.

02

Production Prioritization

Challenge

As the project evolved, the team needed to decide what to improve, simplify, and stop building.

My Response

Used client feedback and playtest findings to prioritize readability, pacing, onboarding, UI clarity, and technical risk.

Outcome

Clearer feedback, better pacing, and stronger onboarding — improvements shipped within the same sprint.

03

Cross-Discipline Alignment

Challenge

VR interaction, UI, level design, and art pipelines were tightly connected, creating many dependencies.

My Response

Shared documentation, task boards, and asset tracking kept progress, blockers, and ownership visible across all disciplines.

Outcome

The team stayed aligned on priorities and delivery targets throughout production.

Leadership Reflection

What I Carry Forward

01

Scope as a Strategic Tool

Scope management is about protecting the experience, not just cutting features. An MVP-first approach keeps the core loop visible and gives teams a shared foundation for evaluating trade-offs.

02

Prioritization Through Evidence

Feedback is useful when it supports clear decisions. I evaluate input through player impact, effort, and risk — focusing on changes that improve the experience without disrupting delivery.

03

Visibility Creates Alignment

Cross-discipline work needs shared visibility. Clear ownership, dependencies, and blockers let each team stay autonomous while remaining aligned.
